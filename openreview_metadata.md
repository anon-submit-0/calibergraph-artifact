## Title

CaliberGraph: Coverage-Caliber Witness Compilation for Multi-Business-Domain Governed Metrics

## Abstract

Industrial text-to-SQL and schema-linking systems increasingly solve where to look, but governed business intelligence must also prove that an answer is valid under metric scope, hierarchy grain, physical coverage, time, and disclosure policy. We call failures after candidate discovery metric-caliber errors. We introduce MetricCaliberBench, a public blind/gold protocol for this missing axis, and CaliberGraph, which compiles governance artifacts into six executable constraint families and admits an answer only when a coverage-caliber witness exists. Exact compiler scores of 1.000 are fixed-contract references, not universal victories. Complete runnable controls bound the claim: real MetricFlow reaches 0.625 joint (0.781 with gold metric identity); shared-validator feedback replanning reaches 0.969 on a preregistered 391-case scope, 0.879 on a later full enterprise-text extension, and 0.971 on full MultiGov-510, but leaves validator-invisible candidate-selection errors; and, on the same MultiGov-200 cases, verbatim-contract DeepSeek, Opus, and GPT-5.5 reach 0.865, 0.985, and 1.000. The strongest model can therefore emulate the contract, but requires roughly 115k prompt tokens and 7.24 seconds per request; CaliberGraph matches its reference with zero online calls, 0.058 ms median finalization, and typed witnesses or failure certificates. Three independent practitioners recover released labels with Fleiss kappa 0.968, and replacing all four majority disagreements preserves every method ranking. The contribution is thus not an intrinsic LLM-impossibility claim, but by-construction, model-independent and auditable governed finalization.
